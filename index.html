- My Story

Becoming a software developer was not always my dream. I wanted to work in fashion like every other 16 year old girl out there. Specifically, I wanted to design magazine layouts for major magazines. You know Vouge, Nylon, W etc. In the 90's, early 2000's the inter-web wasn't something I really thought about. Tearing out magazine pages and collaging your bedroom wall was all the rage. Once I entered college, I dove into business, focusing on marketing and branding. This was a way for me to express both my logical, analytical side and creative side. Shortly after college I landed a marketing job and was the event coordinator for 49 stores in 50 states. However, during my time in marketing I saw so many ways in which we could improve our site, cause lets face it first impressions and visuals are everything when it comes to locking in new costumers. A few months later I found myself neck deep in a 3 month full stack coding bootcamp. After I graduated from the program I became a mentor for up and coming developers. In addition to mentoring, I freelance. I love taking on projects that challenge my front end skills and improve my development. Itâ€™s funny how life comes full circle. In high school I didn't even know that becoming a developer and building websites was something I could do. That 16 year old me finally gets to live out her dream of creating and building layouts. Not yet for the fashion industry but give me time. As a front end web developer I get to combine my creativity, love for technology, marketing, branding and business all into one profession.
